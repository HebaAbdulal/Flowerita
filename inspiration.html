Flower facts & Care advice

Here's how to make your roses last a long time

Roses are a beloved choice for bouquets, fitting for any occasion. With a touch of extra care and affection, you can ensure they stay vibrant for longer. Here, we offer straightforward tips to help you succeed in this endeavor!

Selecting the Perfect Roses

When shopping for roses at flower stores, pick those that are about to bloom. Roses with tightly closed buds may find it challenging to open up, particularly during the winter season.

Keep them hydrated

Roses tend to be delicate and dislike prolonged periods without water. When roses go without water, their stems may draw in air and become obstructed with tiny air bubbles. Typically, florists provide them with water to sustain them on the trip home. However, if roses are left without water for more than 30 minutes, simply trim the stems before placing them in water again to resolve the issue.

Fresh Cuts

Before placing the roses in a vase, it's crucial to trim the stems with a sharp and clean knife. Make a long diagonal cut without crushing the stem.

Clean Vase

Bacteria can shorten the lifespan of roses. Therefore, it's important to use a clean vase with fresh water. Remove any leaves that may be submerged to prevent bacteria from thriving in the water.

Cold Water

Previously, it was believed that roses should be placed in warm water, but according to recent findings, roses prefer cold water. Fill the vase with plenty of water and replenish daily with fresh cold water.

Proper Placement

Avoid placing your roses in drafts, heat, or direct sunlight. Aim to keep them cool and slightly shaded. If possible, you can place them in the fridge or cellar overnight.
